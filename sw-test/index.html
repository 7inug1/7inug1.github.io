Q. How do you use it?

Expand/Collapse

1. Turn on the web camera by clicking 'On' button

You can also turn it off by clicking the 'Off' button.

2. Click 'Capture' button to save the still image

The image will be saved to the canvas on the right and local storage.

3. Draw on the photo captured

Click and hold to keep drawing. Short click to draw dots.

4. Get yourself disconnected from the Internet and refresh the page!

Service worker and Cache running at the back allows you to have offline experience.

Q. What is it comprised of?

Expand/Collapse

1. Status Bar

Status Bar at the top of the page indicates whether there is an online connection or not. When it's online, it is indicated with a green bar and when it's not, it is indicated with a red bar.

2. Webcam & Canvas

You will see two square boxes located in the middle of the page. The one on the left is a web camera. To your right is a canvas that works as an image holder, also as a drawing canvas.

3. Service Worker

The easy way to describe it is that it allows you to have an offline experience. In a more expert way, it is one of the web workers that acts as a proxy server. Here is the reason why I am using it.
