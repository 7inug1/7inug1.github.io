Instruction

1. Turn on the web camera by clicking 'On' button

You can also turn it off by clicking the 'Off' button.

2. Click 'Capture' button to save the still image

The image will be saved to the canvas on the right and local storage.

3. Draw on the photo captured

Click and hold to keep drawing. Short click to draw dots.

4. Get yourself disconnected from the Internet and refresh the page!

Service worker and Cache running at the back allows you to have offline experience.


HTML Components

1. On/Offline Eventlistener

Status Bar at the top of the page uses online/offline event listeners to indicates whether there is an online connection or not.

2. Webcam & Canvas

You will see two square boxes located in the middle of the page. On your left is a web camera. To your right is a canvas that works as an image holder. You can also draw on it.

3. Service Worker

The easy way to describe it is that it allows you to have an offline experience. In a more expert way, it is one of the web workers that acts as a proxy server.

4. Local Storage

Local storage is in use to store data on your local browser. It is better than using cookie in a way that it is more secure and larger amount of data (about 5MB) can be stored.
